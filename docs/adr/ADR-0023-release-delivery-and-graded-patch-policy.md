# ADR-0023 — Release delivery, graded patchability, and a security-patch fast-lane onto modified installs

**Status:** Accepted (Lars, 2026-06-17). Research parent: [`../../research/r4-update-and-delivery.md`](../../research/runtime/r4-update-and-delivery.md) (closes BACKLOG R4 — the research backlog is now empty). **Composes with** [ADR-0017](ADR-0017-fork-upgrade-survival.md) (fork-merge survival — this adds the delivery channel and the security-patch lane on top of its preflight) and [ADR-0010](ADR-0010-cra-conformity-and-secure-sdlc.md) / A27 (conformity transfers to the modifier of a fork). Builds on [ADR-0002](ADR-0002-source-customization-over-runtime-config.md) (seam discipline, M1–M4, policy seams). Sharpens P7/P4 and the `06 §6.9` patch-SLA.

## Context

R4's headline was "how do releases reach each deployment," but the load-bearing question is sharper: **how do we patch a deployment, especially with a security fix, when the customer has already modified the source (P2/ADR-0002) — and how much modification can we still guarantee patchability around?**

The repo already commits the *merge-time* half. ADR-0017 freezes seam interfaces (evolve via `V2` + capability probe), splits migrations into `core/` (ours) and `custom/` (theirs) with `x_`-prefixed custom columns, mandates expand/contract, and ships the `crm gen upgrade` preflight that classifies every conflict into three buckets — marked-region (clean), shared-function ("UNION, DON'T PICK"), core-override (RED, human-required) — and refuses to merge unless fixtures stay green. The spike proved the edges: additive customization and a real conflicting upgrade PASS (probes 1–2); overriding a core invariant has no safe seam and FAILS (probe 3). A27 already says a substantial modification makes the modifier the CRA "manufacturer" of their version.

What was missing: the *delivery* policy (how the signed artifact physically arrives per tier) and the *security-patch lane onto an already-modified install* (so a fix doesn't require taking a whole feature upgrade), plus a customer-facing **grade boundary** that says how much modification keeps the guarantee. The whole industry answer is one shape — keep changes out of the patched files (WordPress child themes, Odoo `_inherit`, SuiteCRM `custom/`, Salesforce namespaced packages) and backport security fixes as small isolated diffs (Linux distros). Margince already has the separation half; this ADR adds the backport-lane half and grades the promise.

## Decision

**Patchability is graded by how far a fork stays inside the seams; we guarantee the two in-seam grades; a security fix ships as an isolated diff cherry-pickable onto a modified install; every tier consumes the same signed artifact over a tier-appropriate channel.**

1. **Three patchability grades, each a promise** (named onto the preflight's existing conflict buckets):
   - **Green — in-seam / additive** (new `x_` columns, `custom/` migrations, new files, the curated policy-seam strategies of ADR-0002). Touches no core file a patch touches. **Guaranteed: clean, mechanical patch application** via the fast-lane below.
   - **Yellow — shared-function touch** (`toDTO`/`Validate`/`applyTo` edits). **Guaranteed but supervised:** the preflight's "UNION, DON'T PICK" + the M1 contract round-trip test + a green fixture run apply the patch with no silent field-drop.
   - **Red — core-invariant override** (probe-3 class). **Not guaranteed.** The modifier is the CRA "manufacturer" (A27) and resolves it themselves or buys the consulting fix (P14). The policy-seam set exists to pull common needs out of Red into Green.
2. **A security-patch fast-lane.** Security fixes are published as a **small isolated diff against core** (plus a patch release), **cherry-pickable onto current + the two prior minors and the ESR** without a full feature upgrade (the distro security-backport model). `crm gen upgrade` gains a **patch-only mode** that classifies the incoming diff Green/Yellow/Red against the fork and applies it accordingly.
3. **One signed artifact, channel by tier.** Each release = immutable container images + a single binary, **cosign keyless-signed (Sigstore/Fulcio/Rekor) + a SLSA build-provenance attestation + the CycloneDX SBOM** (A21), verification mandatory before apply (K8s tiers enforce at admission). Docker Content Trust is not adopted (retired Aug 2025). Channels by deployment mode (`06 §6.3`; **Gradion publishes the signed artifact, the operator applies it** — A35/ADR-0027, Gradion runs no hosting): **partner-hosted** continuous-deploy (the **hosting partner** pushes, no client version); **dedicated/on-prem managed** managed-push on a schedule by the operator; **air-gapped** a signed **offline bundle** into the client's private registry mirror; **source-delivered fork** client-pull a signed tag consumed by the preflight.
4. **Cadence, support window, security SLA.** Monthly stable minor + scheduled/ad-hoc patch releases; an **ESR/LTS track** (~9-month line, 12-month support overlap) for regulated/on-prem; security backports to **current + 2 prior** minors, with the ESR carrying the CRA 10-year-class tail. Severity targets **Critical 72h / High 7d / Medium 30d / Low 90d**; actively-exploited / CISA-KEV-class on the **CRA 24h** fast-path (the A21 ENISA notification). We *publish* within target; the client *applies* per their change window (the air-gapped split, `06 §6.8` client-owns-DR). Numbers are starting anchors, recalibrated on dogfood (like A3/A7).
5. **This tool is also the license-enforcement point (A37/ADR-0029, BUSL per-seat).** Because the per-seat charge applies on self-host and the code is open (a runtime key check is strippable), enforcement is moved to the **server side of this delivery channel**: the website issues a **license key** (encoding seat entitlement); the `crm gen upgrade`/release client — **shipped as a closed-source, signed binary, not part of the BUSL-open source tree** — **presents the key** to Gradion's release service to fetch signed updates + security patches + the **CRA conformity attestation** (A33); **the service validates the key + seat entitlement server-side and serves only to a valid, in-entitlement key** (the gate runs on our infra — un-strippable). A missing/over-seat key is refused or flagged; the instance reports actual active seats at update time for **seat true-up** (the GitLab/Elastic self-managed pattern). **Air-gapped:** a signed, time-boxed **offline license token** issued from the website, validated against Gradion's public key, expiring and needing re-issue. The compliance hook does the real work: stripping the key forfeits signed patches + the attestation, which fails a regulated customer's own CRA/NIS2 audit. **Build item at WP13** (layered on this tool, not a new system; may lag first releases — the license permits the charge from day one, the metering machinery is built when self-host-at-scale appears). Full design: `12-license.md §6.1`.

## Consequences

- **Positive:** turns "can you patch us after we changed it?" into a legible, enforceable promise (Green/Yellow guaranteed, Red owned by the modifier) that rides machinery we already specced (ADR-0017 preflight buckets); the security fast-lane gets a critical fix onto a modified fork without re-validating a whole feature release; one signed artifact gives every tier (incl. air-gapped/regulated) the same integrity story a TISAX/CRA audit wants; no new datastore or vendor.
- **Negative / costs:** the patch-only preflight lane and the ESR backport line are real build/maintenance work (release engineering becomes a first-class function); the air-gapped Critical SLA is split (we publish, they apply) and depends on their change window; Red forks get no guarantee, which must be communicated honestly up front, not discovered during an incident; cadence/SLA numbers are unvalidated until dogfood.
- **Open questions (→ `07-risks.md`):** the custom-column drift-guard is still an open implementation spike (ADR-0017's Atlas `exclude` caveat) and the Green guarantee leans on it; exact ESR interval and the per-severity numbers pending dogfood; whether to ever offer live/hot-patching (out for V1).

## What this does NOT change (guardrails)

- The ADR-0017 seam model (frozen seams + `V2` probes, `core/`+`custom/` migrations, `x_` columns, expand/contract, the preflight, the structured CHANGELOG) — unchanged; this names a delivery channel and a patch-only lane on top of it.
- The ADR-0002 customization paradigm and its curated policy seams — unchanged; the grades are the patch-time expression of the same seam discipline.
- A27 / ADR-0010 conformity transfer — reinforced; Red = the modifier is the manufacturer of their version.
- The three deployment modes (`06 §6.3`) and the client-owns-DR stance (`06 §6.8`) — reinforced; the channels are the per-mode delivery of the same codebase.
- The BUSL grant (`customization-portability.md`) — unchanged; the customer holds and can always patch the core they are entitled to.
