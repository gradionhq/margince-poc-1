# ADR-0025 — EU compliance posture: AI Act · GDPR · NIS2-as-supplier · SBOM/CRA as one issue

**Status:** Accepted (Lars, 2026-06-17). Research parent: [`../../research/r5-eu-compliance-posture.md`](../../research/runtime/r5-eu-compliance-posture.md) (closes BACKLOG **R5**; R6–R8 remain open). **Composes with** [ADR-0010](ADR-0010-cra-conformity-and-secure-sdlc.md) + A27 (CRA conformity + manufacturer transfer — the supplier-attestation backbone), [ADR-0011](ADR-0011-consent-and-retention.md) + A22 (per-purpose consent + retention), [ADR-0023](ADR-0023-release-delivery-and-graded-patch-policy.md) + A31 (signed delivery + graded patchability — the "patchable on the customer's clock" half of the NIS2 supplier duty). Builds on A8 (egress location ladder), A13 (erasure/suppression/tombstone), A28/[ADR-0020](ADR-0020-customer-supplied-inference.md) (customer-supplied inference → Margince is not a GPAI provider). **Resolves the two open items A24 recorded.** New decision: **DECISIONS A33**. *(Not to be confused with [ADR-0024](ADR-0024-dach-primary-gtm-frame.md) / A32 — the DACH-primary GTM frame, a separate same-day decision.)*

## Context

A24 made EU residency the SaaS default and "sovereign European CRM" a positioning pillar, but **recorded two items as open, not deferred**: a formal **EU AI Act risk-tier classification of the CRM's features + a DPIA**, and a **NIS2 scoping** determination. R5 resolves both, and Lars sharpened them:

- The AI Act matters **because Margince scores people and companies** (lead score, relationship strength, brief ranking, contact warm/blocking labels) — profiling of natural persons is the real regulatory edge, not a checkbox.
- **NIS2 is about the customer, not Gradion.** Gradion-the-company is treated as out of scope; the load-bearing question is what Margince must provide *as a supplier* so a NIS2-regulated customer can run it compliantly.
- SBOM/CRA (A21/A27/A31) and GDPR (A8/A13/A22) are not separate topics — they are the supply-chain and data-protection faces of the **same** compliance surface.

The exploration confirmed the spec already shipped ~70% of the *mechanical* work (consent engine, retention + legal-hold, erasure + suppression + tombstone, SBOM + CVD + secure-SDLC + signed releases, DPA + sub-processor list, egress location ladder, "Explain this score", the RED-removed biometric/emotion guards). What was missing was the **legal classification, the DPIA, the AI-transparency layer, the contact-facing data-subject path, and one customer-presentable bundle** — plus the NIS2 reframe.

## Decision

**Margince's AI features are limited-risk / minimal-risk under the EU AI Act (not high-risk); the sharper regime is GDPR profiling + AI-transparency, already mitigated by the human-in-the-loop, evidence-or-omit, no-biometrics design; NIS2 is a supplier obligation answered by the CRA/SBOM artifacts plus an incident-notification commitment; and the whole posture is bundled into one downloadable supplier-conformity pack.** Five parts:

1. **EU AI Act = limited/minimal-risk + Art. 50 transparency.** No feature maps to an Annex III high-risk use case (not creditworthiness, not worker evaluation, not essential-service eligibility, not biometrics/emotion — the last RED-removed in `features/07 §11`). The only binding duty is **Art. 50 transparency**: disclose AI interactions (the assistant surface) and mark AI-generated/assisted content (drafts, brief, dossier, voice drafts). Margince is the **provider**; the customer is the **deployer**; inference is customer-supplied (A28) so Margince is **not a GPAI provider**.

2. **GDPR is the load-bearing regime, and the design already passes it.** Scoring is **profiling**, but every consequential action is **staged behind a 🟡 human gate** and scores are advisory, so there is **no Art. 22 solely-automated decision with legal or similarly significant effect**. The Art. 22(3)/13/14/15 "meaningful logic" surface is the already-built **"Explain this score"** (`features/03 §3.4`) + **"no mystery number"** (`features/07 §4`) + **evidence-or-omit** (`features/07 §11`). Profiling lawful basis rides the per-purpose consent engine's `profiling` purpose (A22). **Two gaps recorded:** (a) the privacy notice must state **AI processing** of captured content (Art. 13/14) — a notice-content requirement on the existing `policy_text`/`policy_version` proof fields; (b) **contact-facing SAR** (Art. 15 for a contact, not only a CRM user) is currently fast-follow — **promotion to V1 is a scope call surfaced for Lars, not assumed.**

3. **The DPIA is drafted now**, not deferred: [`../compliance/DPIA.md`](../compliance/DPIA.md), keyed to the scoring/profiling inventory, with an Art. 22 analysis per feature and a risk→mitigation register reusing consent default-deny, the egress ladder, the RED-removed guards, audit, 🟡 gates, and erasure/suppression. It is a draft for **counsel review before the CE mark** (the pre-existing `06 §6.9` gate).

4. **NIS2 is a supplier duty, answered by what we already ship.** A NIS2-regulated customer owes supply-chain security (Art. 21(2)(d)) and incident reporting (Art. 23: 24h/72h/1-month). Margince meets the supplier side by being **attestable** (SBOM + DoC + secure-SDLC evidence + signed/attested releases — A21/A31), **patchable on their clock** (graded patchability + security-patch fast-lane + per-tier delivery — A31/ADR-0023), and **prompt to notify** — a new **supplier incident-notification commitment**: notify affected customers of a relevant incident/vulnerability without undue delay, aligned to the CRA 24h actively-exploited fast-path, so their own 24h/72h clock is feasible. *(Gradion-the-company NIS2 scoping — incl. the managed-security-practice MSSP question — is parked as a separate company determination, re-run if the practice scales past the size threshold; it does not change the product.)*

5. **One supplier-conformity pack.** A downloadable **EU compliance pack** = SBOM (CycloneDX) + DoC/CE self-assessment + CVD policy/`security.txt` + signed-release/SLSA provenance + the incident-notification commitment + sub-processor list + DPA + DPIA template + the Art. 50 transparency statement. Kept current per release, public where the open core allows. A modified fork re-attests its own pack delta (A27, billable, P14). This is the single artifact a customer's NIS2/CRA/GDPR auditor asks for, and the dogfood reference for the CRA-readiness consulting practice.

## Consequences

- **Closes A24's two open items.** `07-risks.md` strikes them; `06 §6.5` lands the classification and reframes NIS2 to the supplier duty; `06 §6.9` and `features/04 §6` add the supplier pack + incident-notification SLA.
- **One genuinely new product obligation:** the Art. 50 transparency layer (`features/07` drafts/brief/dossier; `03b`/`03c` assistant disclosure) — additive labelling on an already-honest, draft-only, evidence-or-omit surface.
- **Two recorded gaps to close in build:** the AI-processing privacy-notice line, and the contact-facing SAR scope decision.
- **No architecture change.** Everything reuses existing machinery; the dividend of A28 (no GPAI exposure) and A21/A31 (the NIS2 supplier evidence already exists) is realised, not rebuilt.
- **Counsel-gated.** The limited-risk and Category-1 classifications, and the incident-notification SLA number, are confirmed with counsel before the CE mark — consistent with `06 §6.9`.
- **No PDF regen** — none of the swept docs are in the PDF build set.

## Amendment 1 (2026-06-19) — the certificate/attestation layer: who holds what (Gradion runs no cloud)

ADR-0025 settled the **legal** posture (AI Act/GDPR/NIS2/CRA). It did not address the **certificate/attestation** layer (BSI C5, ISO 27001, SOC 2, TISAX, EUCS), which surfaced via the partner-program work ([`../14-partner-program.md`](../../business/14-partner-program.md), A38). Verified research ([`../../research/eu-certificates.md`](../../research/eu-certificates.md), 2026-06-19, primary BSI/EUR-Lex/EC/ENISA sources) resolves it. **Obligations attach to ROLE, not to who wrote the code — and the A35 "no hosting" decision is what assigns them:**

- **CRA = Gradion's, as manufacturer.** Gradion develops + markets the software under its own name, so Gradion is the CRA **manufacturer** regardless of who hosts: self-assessed conformity (the CRM is **default-tier** — not Annex III/IV — so **no notified body, no mandatory EU cyber-cert**), technical file, EU DoC, **CE mark on the DoC/website**, Art. 14 vuln/incident reporting. **Dates (verified):** CRA in force 10 Dec 2024; **Art. 14 reporting from 11 Sep 2026; CE/main obligations from 11 Dec 2027.** (A substantial-modifier fork becomes the manufacturer of its version — Art. 21, ties to A27.)
- **BSI C5 = the OPERATOR's, never Gradion's.** C5 is an **attestation** (ISAE 3000, per *cloud service*), not a company certificate, and its controls are operational — **only whoever runs the service can hold it.** Since Gradion hosts nothing (A35), C5 belongs to the **hosting partner** (or self-hosting customer). It is **legally mandatory for the operator processing statutory-health data** (**§393 SGB V**, in force 1 Jul 2024: C5 Type 1 → Type 2 from 1 Jul 2025) and contractually expected in public-sector/finance. **EUCS** (EU cloud scheme) likewise attaches to the operator and is unadopted/stalled (no obligation today).
- **ISO 27001 / SOC 2 / TISAX = optional, organizational — and Gradion ALREADY HOLDS ISO/IEC 27001** (founder, 2026-06-19). Not required for a no-cloud software vendor, but **already in hand** as an org-ISMS trust signal — useful in sales/channel and as credibility to help partners reach C5/ISO. TISAX optional (automotive); SOC 2 not needed in DACH.
- **Gradion's leverage:** the **EU compliance pack** (§5 above) is exactly what a partner needs to pass *its* C5/ISO audit faster — so **Gradion enables the cert without holding it.** That is the channel/sovereignty pitch.

**Net:** Gradion's certificate burden is just the CRA self-assessment it already scoped (A21); the cloud-service certs (C5/EUCS) are pushed to the operator by the no-hosting model. Recorded as a clarification under A33 (no new decision number); partner-side C5 obligation documented in [`../14-partner-program.md`](../../business/14-partner-program.md) §6 and [`licensing/PARTNER-LICENSE.md`](../../business/licensing/PARTNER-LICENSE.md).

## Alternatives considered

- **Split into two ADRs (AI Act separate from GDPR/NIS2/SBOM).** Rejected: Lars's framing is "different angles of the same issue", and the supplier pack only makes sense as one bundle. A single posture is more defensible and easier to hand an auditor.
- **Defer the DPIA to counsel as a future doc.** Rejected: A24 already said "the classification is to be made, with a DPIA, not assumed"; drafting it now closes the item and gives counsel something concrete to red-line.
- **Assume limited-risk silently (as A24 hinted) and move on.** Rejected: the classification is load-bearing for a product sold on sovereignty/compliance; it gets written down with the Annex III walk-through and the counsel gate.
